INTRODUCTION

"Know Your Revenue" is an interactive dashboard I designed for a cloud service company in my first year of graduate study in Simon Fraser University as a research project, to help their customers analyze business data hosted on their cloud more easily. The specific customer is a chain restaurant company, who business is across both US and Canada. They would like to know what factors can affect their revenue, and if there is a much easier way to assist them in making business decisions based on data instead of boring tables or simple Excel charts.Therefore, after months of discussions with the cloud service company, we agreed on the development a new tool to solve their problems. The users of the tool are company managers and their data analysts. The data needed to be analyzed is large and multi-dimensional, and design and development cycle for the tool is about 1 month.

To comply with my non-disclosure agreement, I have omitted confidential information such as names of the two companies.

CHALLENGES & SOLUTIONS

CHALLENGE SOLUTION
Problems with tables & simple charts
The restaurant data was stored as dataframes with shared dimensions across different tables. It was impossible to gain any insights from the complex, multi-dimensional raw data. They also tried to use Excel to generate some simple charts, but it was hard to show the relations between different dimensions.		 Interactive Dashboard
We decided to build a dashboard for KPI related data dimensions, and placed charts with strong relations together in the same view. Interactions included filtering, brushing and linking, meaning that any updates to one chart would affect others.
Looking for the best way to implement
At first, we were trying to use IPython ran on Jupyter Notebook which was already set up in the cloud service company server. Although IPython has some good libraries to handle data and generate visualizations, but customization and flexibiltiy of Jupyter Notebook is limited.		 Web Application
We decided to develop a web application using HTML5/CSS3, and Javascript, which is powerful enough with many libraries such as d3.js & dc.js to create SVG charts. We implemented locally at first, and it would be easy to move the tool to their servers.
Choice of data dimensions and chart types
There were tons of data dimensions in our customers raw data sets, but at the first stage, they only cared about which factors could affect their revenue. We were not familiar with their database at first, and exploring multiple tables with outliers indeed caused us some troubles to define dimensions.		 Revenue Driven Factors
We used IPython to do some data mining work, such as filtering outliers, printing all dimensions related to revenue. And we came up with the list of revenue driven factors that would affect revenue in terms of information from data, and then we held meetings and discuss with the company stuffs from data analysts to CEO to confirm their needs. We decided mainly use bar chart to achieve best comparisons and line chart to show the trend of change.
